Selected Research

Magnetic Tracking of Tongue Movements Based on Ensemble of Regional Perceptrons

Background: The magnetic localization method opens up new and interesting applications in medical diagnosis and therapy, such as capsule endoscopy, tongue tracking, whereas also posing challenges in achieving high positioning accuracy and robustness. Recently, a localization method based on MLP has been proposed, which eliminates the need for parameter calibration using the Euler equation and achieves a favorable localization effect. However, highly non-linear environmental noise will have a significant impact on the MLP. Secondly, the distribution characteristics of the spatial magnetic field generated by the tracer and the ambient magnetic field noise differ in different regions. Overfitting is common when a single MLP is employed to model the entire space. Method: To address these problems, firstly, the gated Multilayer Perceptron(gMLP) is employed to facilitate the model to adaptively suppress environmental interference. Secondly, We designed Spatial-Angular Ensemble (SAE) to significantly improve the robustness of the model to noise. Result: SAE, respectively, achieved an average RMSE of 0.94 mm and 3.71° for tracer location and orientation estimation, which were 60.67% and 39.38% lower than those of MLP. Conclusion: The results show that the proposed SAE has a high potential to improve the localization accuracy and robustness of the system.
